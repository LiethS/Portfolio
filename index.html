    Recent Placement Reflection: Mondelēz International
    Currently I work at Mondelez International as a Maintenance Co-op. My supervisor Santiago Munera, the Maintenance Co-Leader, and I chose that my learning goal for this placement was to get familiarized with the Mondelez food manufacturing process and develop new methods to any approach to optimize the process. Although this is a difficult objective to quantify, we’ve decided that the best way to measure my growth would be to determine how many hours of work I can automate and how much money I can save the company through optimization of processes. To determine if I was successful, we can test any scripts I create to see the time saved as opposed to doing the tasks manually, we can also do some simple calculations to determine how much more money my new method generates as opposed to the previous approach. So far into the placement, I have learned many things. I first started by helping coworkers in the office and getting to know them. I’ve realized that all my coworkers are quite nice and helpful, so I am now comfortable with asking for help or anything if I ever need it. I helped around with some printing jobs as well as some commissioning for some products. Simple but irrelevant stuff so far, but I am having fun. I began using SAP which is a relational database and I’ve become quite proficient in it. I have started working on a project, which involves me reorganizing and standardizing the SAP equipment tree. This helps make everyone’s work much easier as the database becomes much more organized in relation to the reality of the equipment’s states. I have become proficient enough to be able to write scripts automating tedious tasks for PM Managers, saving them many hours daily and many more yearly. I’ve created scripts to automate daily revisions done for every weekday and every day of the weekend. I also created a script to automate the printing and assigning of work orders which was a process taking up to an hour or two from 2-3 members of the maintenance team.
